# Evidence Update Report: Endometrial Cancer Postoperative Management Plan

## Executive Evidence Summary
The core of the provided postoperative management plan is **largely consistent with current (2024-2025) evidence**, but has **critical, practice-changing gaps**. The plan correctly identifies the need to classify low-volume sentinel lymph node (SLN) metastasis and recommends adjuvant chemoradiation for stage III disease, which is supported by high-level data. However, the plan is **incomplete and not fully modern** because it **lacks integration of molecular classification** (POLE, MMR/MSI, p53), which is now a fundamental determinant of adjuvant therapy intensity, especially for node-positive patients. The proposed follow-up regimen includes tumor markers not strongly validated for endometrial cancer surveillance in the provided evidence.

## Annotated Plan Review

### 1. Step 1: 联系病理科明确孤立细胞转移、微转移或宏转移
*   **Status**: [✅ Current]
*   **Latest Evidence**: This step is strongly supported by contemporary evidence. Differentiating between isolated tumor cells (ITCs), micrometastasis, and macrometastasis in SLNs is a critical research and clinical focus. Recent studies specifically evaluate the prognostic significance and impact on adjuvant treatment decisions for these low-volume metastases. [^^5], [^^6], [^^27] A 2024 systematic review further analyzes their prognostic impact within molecular subtypes. [^^25] Clarifying this is essential for precise risk stratification.

### 2. Step 2: 建议辅助：TC方案化疗+EBRT±VB
*   **Status**: [⚠️ Update Available]
*   **Latest Evidence**: The recommendation for adjuvant chemotherapy combined with external beam radiotherapy (EBRT) for stage III (node-positive) disease is supported by long-term data. The 10-year follow-up of the PORTEC-3 trial confirms a sustained benefit of adjuvant chemoradiotherapy over radiotherapy alone in high-risk endometrial cancer. [^^28]
*   **Critical Update Required**: The plan must incorporate **molecular classification**. Current evidence unequivocally states that molecular subtype can override anatomic stage in guiding therapy. Most notably, patients with POLE-mutated tumors, even with SLN-positive disease, have an excellent prognosis, and treatment de-escalation is a key consideration. [^^26] The provided immunochemistry shows MMR proficiency (MLH1+, MSH2+, MSH6+, PMS2+), but POLE mutation status and detailed p53 assessment are missing. Adjuvant therapy intensity should be guided by the integrated molecular risk profile. [^^26], [^^28]

### 3. Step 3: 随访方案
*   **Follow-up Intervals & Imaging**: The suggested frequency (every 3-6 months initially) and the use of imaging based on symptoms or suspicion of recurrence are consistent with general oncologic follow-up principles.
*   **Follow-up Tumor Markers (CA125, HE4, CA199)**:
    *   **Status**: [⚠️ Evidence Not Confirmed]
    *   **Latest Evidence**: The provided API data summary **does not contain specific evidence validating the routine use of CA125, HE4, and CA199 in the surveillance of endometrial cancer**. While CA125 may be elevated in some cases, its role in structured follow-up for endometrial cancer (unlike ovarian cancer) is not standardly evidence-based per the retrieved citations.

### 4. 术后诊断 Staging
*   **Status**: [⚠️ Update Available]
*   **Latest Evidence**: The plan uses the **FIGO 2009** staging system (III C1). The current standard is the **FIGO 2023** staging system, which integrates molecular classification. While the core management for node-positive disease remains relevant, the staging nomenclature should be updated. [^^2]

## New Trials & Approvals
*   The patient's profile (stage III, ER/PR positive) aligns with ongoing research into novel endocrine therapies (e.g., selective estrogen receptor degraders like imlunestrant) for advanced/recurrent disease. [^^20], [^^21] However, these are not yet standard **adjuvant** treatments and represent options for future therapy lines if needed.
